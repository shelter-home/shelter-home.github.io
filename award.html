OUTSTANDING PRIMARY LEARNING CENTRE AWARD 2015

"The United Nations High Commissioner for Refugees (UNHCR) has awarded the "Outstanding Primary Learning Centre" Award to SHELTER Community School 1 (SCS1). SCS1 is one of the five refugee children's schools under SHELTER.

UNHCR has presented awards for refugee learning centres for the secondary, primary and preschool groups. Among the other 125 centres in Malaysia in the Main Category, SCS1 was the winner.

SCS1, in line with its vision of "sheltering the future generation," does not limit its treatment to those races alone, but to all children seeking refuge and assistance.SCS1 uses a more innovative and non-conventional approach to learning for its students. The school also aims to give its students the best education that they can get.


THE INAUGURAL 2004 ASIA PACIFIC NGO AWARDS

SHELTER was the Malaysian winner of the inaugural 2004 Asia Pacific NGO Awards.

SHELTER aims for honesty, trustworthiness, experience and knowledge, and high performance expectations. The Asia Pacific NGO Awards, the theme of which was "celebrating success…. rewarding excellence" attests to SHELTER's professionalism in the field of finance and donation. SHELTER has internal and external checks and balances to ensure that contributions from the public do not go wrong.

The Asia Pacific NGO Awards, organised by the Resource Alliance and Citigroup Asia Pacific and sponsored by the Citigroup Foundation, is the only regional competition of its kind that recognises and rewards the best NGOs/non-profit organisations.The awards, first introduced in 2004, centred on professional management, creativity in local resource mobilisation and the success of the organization's services in enhancing the lives of the people they represent. 


ANUGERAH KHAS 2007

SHELTER was awarded with an ‘Anugerah Khas’ (Special Award) by the Prison Department of Malaysia in conjunction with their launching of National Level Prisons Day celebration at Prison College in Kajang on 4th November 2007

The award was given in recognition of SHELTER's commitment to working with prison authorities and providing prison education services to young prisoners in Kajang Prison.

SHELTER also offered after-release rehabilitation programmes for remanded young prisoners, it covered programmes targeted at keeping minor offenders out of prison, provided treatment to young prisoners who are victims of child abuse and neglect, helped them to employment opportunities, provided counseling for young prisoners and their families and many more, including forming a brass band for young prisoners in prison and a halfway home.

As a token of acknowledgement and recognition of SHELTER's contribution to educating and rehabilitating young prisoners in Kajang Prison, SHELTER is further committed to delivering quality facilities to excellence.
